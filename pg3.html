When it comes to cursive fonts in CSS

,

you can use the cursive value for the font-family property. Here are a few examples of cursive fonts you can use:

Comic Sans MS:

A playful and informal font that emulates handwriting. Example: font-family: 'Comic Sans MS', 'Comic Sans', cursive;

Apple Chancery:

A cursive font with a more elegant and calligraphic feel. Example: font-family: 'Apple Chancery', cursive;

Bradley Hand:

Another cursive font resembling handwritten script. Example: font-family: 'Bradley Hand', cursive;

Brush Script MT:

A brush-style cursive font often used for creative designs. Example: font-family: 'Brush Script MT', 'Brush Script Std', cursive; Remember to adjust the font-size and other properties as needed to achieve the desired look for your text. Cursive fonts can add a touch of personality and flair to your web design!
